A Poem to Miss K, 2024

In a quiet corner, shadows sweep,
Silus, thirteen, begins to weep.
His heart aches with a hollow sound,
For MISS K, now beneath the ground.

A tabby cat with a loud, bold meow,
A stand-offish grace, yet patience endowed.
Proud and regal, she ruled her domain,
Now Silus mourns her, feeling the pain.

MISS K, with fur of sunlit hue,
Stood tall and strong, with eyes of dew.
Her meow, a call both sharp and bright,
Echoes now in the silent night.

She wasn't one to be easily swayed,
Her proud demeanor, her stance displayed.
Yet in her eyes, a love so deep,
A bond with Silus, forever to keep.

In mornings’ light, she’d prowl and prance,
In evening's glow, her patient stance.
With Silus, she shared a quiet peace,
A companionship that would never cease.

No more the nights with her regal tread,
No more her presence beside his bed.
The house now holds a lingering sigh,
As Silus whispers his soft goodbye.

But in his heart, she leaves her mark,
A flame of love that won't grow dark.
Her meows, her pride, her patient gaze,
Live on in memories, a lasting blaze.

He dreams of her in fields of gold,
Where she meows loud, her spirit bold.
In a world where shadows cannot stay,
MISS K prowls, forever in play.

Though tears fall and sorrow weighs,
Silus finds strength in brighter days.
For love like theirs transcends the end,
A bond unbroken, a soul to tend.

MISS K, the cat with the loud, proud sound,
Now watches over, her spirit unbound.
In the stars, in the wind’s soft flow,
Her presence lingers, this he knows.

So he looks to the sky with a tear and a smile,
Knowing she's with him, every mile.
In his heart, her proud meow stays,
A testament to their golden days.
